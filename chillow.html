Project Context

About Chillow

Chillow is a mobile app that allows users to match with potential roommates and housing through a B2B2C model.

About My Role

In addition to overseeing the design and research process, I also collaborated with marketing, sales, and engineering teams to develop product strategies.

Who Are The Users?

Chillow's user base is mostly comprised college students and young professionals. Within this group, there are 4 unique types of user experiences:

  1. Users looking to rent out a place
  2. Users looking for a place, and roommates to share that place
  3. Users looking for a place only
  4. Users looking for roommates to share their current place with

Sign Up Flow

Key Improvements

We revamped Chillow's sign up flow to make it a frictionless process. With an understanding that there are 4 very different user goals, we incorporated branching logic and significantly reduced scroll fatigue.

The colors and heading fonts were also updated for readability. This gave the app a more polished look that was cohesive with the brand colors. The heading was also updated, renaming the process from "Profile" to "Create Account" and changing the button from "Save" to "Continue".

Usability Testing

Working with a team that had little to no UX background, I learned to be a strong user advocate. To do this, I utilized data, such as WCAG standards and user feedback to drive my ideas. I got buy-in for usability testing and led the entire process, which included creating a user research plan, highlighting success metrics, and gathering participants.

I conducted 15 user testing sessions with participants using Zoom and prototyped designs on Figma. KPIs that were tracked during the process include time on task, user satisfaction rate, and task success rate. I compiled notes from each session to find patterns, which were synthesized into recommended improvements that I presented to my team.

Next Steps

The sign up flow updates are currently in beta testing for further improvements. As a part of next steps, we hope to gather more feedback through user analytics when it goes live. The projected change is a 10-15% reduction in user dropoff rate, which we are extremely excited for!
